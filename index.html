CHANEL - Men's cologne & Fragrance

Called Coco in 1910, she opens a hat boutique named Chanel Modes at 21 rue Cambon.
Her hat was worn by well-known French actresses at the time, and she built up her fame to open a boutique store.
Chanel No. 5 was the first perfume released by French couturier Gabriel "Coco" Chanel in 1921. It has since released several women's fragrances.
And in 1955, the first perfume for men was created, Pour Monsieur. The fresh chypre scent revealed lemon top notes, spicy middle notes, and a woody trail.
It is the only men's perfume released while Coco Chanel was alive.

In 1981, the second men's perfume, Antaeus, was released. The perfume contains the myth of a hero who is weak but strong with an intense and subtle scent of leather.
A powerful and sensitive symbol of masculinity.

In 1990, the third men's perfume, the Egoiste, was released. The original perfume breaks the code of men's perfume with sandalwood and Madagascar vanilla notes.
Jean-Paul Goode's advertising film won the Golden Lion at the 37th International Advertising Film Festival in Cannes.
